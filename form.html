PAKHWAJ-a brief information

The pakhwaj is a barrel-shaped, two-headed drum, originating from the indian subcontinent, the oldest version of double sided drums and its descendants are Mridangam of Southern India and kendang of Maritime Southeast Asia and other south Asian double-headed drums. Its older forms were made with clay.

A Deeper Insight

This is the percussion Instrument most commonly used in the Dhrupad style of Indian Classical music, Often being used as a component for many different forms of dances like Kathak, Odissi and Marathi formal Dances.
It has a low mellow tone that is rich in harmonics. The pakhwaj is made with animal skin ,often goat or cow skin. We should note that, there is no killing or torturing animals for the sake of skin.

Parts of Pakhwaj


Pakhwaj is made up of many special parts which plays an important role in acoustics and sound quality of the pakhwaj. The main parts are
Dayan: Syahi, Maidan, chat, Gajara or pagri, pudi
Bayan: atta, Tasma or chot and gatta. Now let us study these parts individually.

    • Dayan:
    This is the right face of the pakhwaj. A black paste if applied which is known as Syahi. The main skin is called maidan. The outer skin is called chat. The braided ring is called Gajara or pagri and the whole drum face is called pudi.

      Bayan:
    • This is the left face of the pakhwaj. A wheat flour paste if applied which is known as atta. The tensioning strap is called maidan. The outer skin is called tasma or chot. The tuning dowels is called Gatta.

    Sounds of Pakhwaj: Basic bols

    There are many high pitched and low pitched sounds that can be played on both Dayan and Bayan respectively. Some of those are:-

      Dayan:
    NA , THAAP / TA , DIN , TE , RE , DHET , TIN
      Bayan:
    GA / GHA / GHE , KA / KHA / KAT

    Combination Bols

    1) DHA = THA + GHE
    2) LA = NA + GHE
    3) DHIN = TIN + GHE
    4) THA = TA + KAT
    5) TIN = TIN + KAT
